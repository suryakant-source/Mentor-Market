How a Retired CFO Helped a Mumbai Startup Raise ₹8 Crore in 60 Days

Rajesh Kumar
Expert in Finance & Fundraising
PayFlow Technologies January 2026

The Challenge

When Priya Sharma founded PayFlow Technologies in 2023, she had a product that worked, a team that believed in the vision, and a burning problem: the company needed ₹8 crore to scale production and close enterprise contracts before a competitor launched a similar product in the market.

But Priya had never raised capital before. Her background was in engineering, not finance. She had heard horror stories of founders spending months building pitch decks only to get rejected by investors for having unrealistic projections or unclear unit economics.

The Turning Point

Priya connected with Rajesh Kumar through MentorMarket. Rajesh had spent 25 years in finance leadership roles at three unicorn startups, including as CFO at a fintech company that eventually IPO'd. He'd recently retired and was looking for ways to share what he'd learned.

"In our first session, Priya showed me her financial model. It was clear she had the intuition for what makes a business work, but the numbers weren't telling the right story for investors," Rajesh recalled. "We spent three sessions completely rebuilding the financial model from scratch — not to manipulate numbers, but to tell the truth in a way that investors could act on."

"The financial model Rajesh helped me build wasn't about making the numbers look good. It was about understanding which metrics actually matter to investors in my space — and being ruthlessly honest about where we were and where we were going."

— Priya Sharma, Founder & CEO, PayFlow Technologies

The Strategy

Rajesh's approach was methodical. First, they analyzed the company's existing customer data to identify the unit economics that actually worked. Then they built a model that showed exactly how the company would reach profitability — and what milestones would justify the valuation Priya was asking for.

They also worked on the pitch deck itself. "Most founders try to hide weaknesses. Experienced investors see through that immediately," Rajesh said. "We positioned the challenges honestly and showed exactly how we planned to overcome them. That authenticity landed better than any polished fiction ever could."

By the sixth week, PayFlow had a financial model, a pitch deck, and a shortlist of 12 investors who were the right fit for a company at their stage. Priya started scheduling meetings.

The Outcome

Within 60 days of starting the process with Rajesh, PayFlow Technologies closed a ₹8 crore Series A round from two prominent VC firms. The lead investor specifically cited the quality of the financial model and the clarity of the growth strategy as reasons for their confidence in the deal.

Today, PayFlow has deployed that capital to scale their engineering team, sign three enterprise contracts worth ₹15 crore in total contract value, and begin the hiring process for a full finance function — a role Priya says they would have struggled to justify without Rajesh's early guidance.
